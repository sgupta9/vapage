What to expect

  • Awaken your self

    We need a strong intellect to make the right choices in our lives. Knowledge from schools and universities provide us no relief from despair, depression, stress and strain. To be productive and peaceful let your intellect control the mind and get ahead with action.

  • Make better choices?

    Life constantly presents us choices. Hamlet's famous lines - "to be or not to be" highlights this dilemma of choice. On what basis do we choose? Are we victims to our feelings and circumstances which toss us around or should we develop our intellect to rise above them.

  • Discover more clarity

    To win, know the rules of the game. A powerful intellect alone can overcome challenges of life. Assert control over circumstance. You are the architect of your fortune and misfortune. If you want to win at life, this is it.

The Speakers

Swami Parthasarathy

Known as the "Go-Getter's Guru", Swami A. Parthasarathy is featured in leading publications such as TIME, Business Week, Forbes, CNN, Sports Illustrated and the Sunday Times.

  • Author of twelve books including three bestsellers in six decades of study and research on Vedanta.
  • Has addressed the World Bank, Festival of thinkers, Young Presidents' Organisation, Indian Cricket Team, Harvard, Stanford, Oxford, NASA, Google, Walt Disney et al.
  • Founder of the Vedanta Academy.
  • At age 93, continues to play cricket and win man-of-the-match awards competing alongside players less than a third his age.

Sunandaji

Sunandaji is the daughter-disciple of Swami A. Parthasarathy.

  • Sunandaji has been studying and researching Vedanta, the ancient philosophy of India, for decades.
  • Has addressed diverse audiences across four continents for the past forty years including Young Presidents' Organisation, Harvard Business School, EY.
  • Sunandaji's clear and powerful presentation of ancient wisdom in contemporary thought has received widespread coverage in television, radio and print media.

Alumni of the Vedanta Academy

Alumni of the Vedanta Academy's three-year residential course who are dedicated to the full-time study and research of Vedanta. With over a decade of teaching experience they have addressed audiences around the world.
